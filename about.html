Curious by nature.
Rigorous by practice.

I'm Grant Thomas — a UX Designer and Product Strategist based in Brooklyn, NY. For over a decade I've been turning complicated systems into experiences that actually make sense to the humans using them.

My path to UX design started in an unexpected place: exploring settings pages in video games as a kid. I was fascinated by how small interface changes could dramatically impact the experience. Growing up immersed in digital worlds taught me to recognize what works — and more importantly, what doesn't — in user interfaces.

For the past 9+ years I've been a Senior UX Design Lead on Eli Lilly's design system team (via Coforge, formerly Allegient, DMI, and Encora), working across one of the most complex design system environments in the pharmaceutical industry. Across that tenure I've held nearly every role on the team — researcher, designer, Scrum Master, Product Owner, lead library architect, and lead designer. That breadth isn't accidental; I've always believed you make better design decisions when you understand what surrounds them.

Some of the work I'm most proud of: helping build Lilly.com from the ground up, partnering with Wieden+Kennedy to guide Lilly's new brand color toward an accessible, competitively differentiated system, and building out a versatile HCT-based color system that now spans Lilly's global brand portfolio — Mounjaro, Zepbound, Taltz, Basaglar, and more. The Lilly Design System I work on today serves not just their public web presence but internal applications across their entire global workforce.

My approach centers on discernment, curiosity, and groundedness. I start every project with deep discovery — understanding all the inputs, constraints, and context before jumping to solutions. I'm passionate about accessibility and working directly with developers to ensure designs are both beautiful and feasible in production. I don't want to toss work "over the wall" — I thrive in collaborative environments where I stay engaged through the entire build.

Outside of work, I'm a boulderer, a reader, and a traveler who's happiest when I'm somewhere unfamiliar. I also have a minor in art history and a long-standing practice of street photography inspired by Vivian Maier and Henri Cartier-Bresson. This fall I'm heading to France to complete my yoga teacher training — a commitment to both the practice and the kind of intentional discomfort that tends to produce growth.
